Ibadan North — Multi-Index Land Surface Mapping

A practice run through four of the most common spectral indices in remote sensing — vegetation, soil-adjusted vegetation, water, and built-up — generated entirely in Google Earth Engine for Ibadan North in 2017.

TypePersonal project / practice exercise
LocationIbadan North LGA, Oyo State, Nigeria
Year2017
SoftwareGoogle Earth Engine (JavaScript API)
IndicesNDVI, SAVI, NDWI, NDBI

Overview

Spectral indices turn raw multispectral bands into a single number per pixel that highlights a specific land surface property — how green something is, how wet, how built-up. This exercise was about getting comfortable computing and interpreting four of the standard ones in Google Earth Engine for the same area and date, then reading them side by side rather than in isolation, since indices tend to make a lot more sense in contrast with each other than alone.

The four indices

  • NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) — the standard measure of vegetation greenness and health; higher values mean denser, healthier vegetation.
  • SAVI (Soil-Adjusted Vegetation Index) — a variant of NDVI that corrects for soil brightness showing through sparse canopy, generally more reliable than NDVI in areas with exposed or lightly vegetated soil.
  • NDWI (Normalized Difference Water Index) — highlights surface moisture and water content; values closer to zero indicate more moisture, more negative values indicate drier surfaces.
  • NDBI (Normalized Difference Built-up Index) — flags impervious, built-up surfaces; positive values lean built-up, negative values lean vegetated or moist.
PLATE 01NDVI — vegetation is concentrated in the northwest and around scattered green pockets through the built-up core; the southern half reads consistently low.
PLATE 02SAVI — the same broad pattern as NDVI but visibly compressed, the soil-brightness correction pulling sparsely vegetated pixels down rather than letting bare soil read as moderate vegetation.
PLATE 03NDWI — every pixel in the scene reads negative, meaning no open water in this extent; the teal zones are relatively moist surfaces rather than literal water.
PLATE 04NDBI — the built-up signal sits mostly in the low-positive (pale orange) range across the urban core, with the clearest green low-NDBI patches matching the same vegetated zones visible in the NDVI map.

Reading them together

The real value of this exercise was lining the four up against each other rather than reading any one in isolation. The patches that read high on NDVI and SAVI are the same patches that read low (vegetated) on NDBI — which is exactly the cross-check you'd want, since it confirms the indices are internally consistent rather than just visually similar by coincidence. NDWI staying negative across the whole scene is also informative on its own: it confirms this extent of Ibadan North has no significant open water body, so any future analysis using this data wouldn't need a separate water mask.
