Ibarapa East — Drainage Basin Delineation

A self-directed exercise in DEM-based hydrological analysis: delineating the sub-basins and stream network of Ibarapa East from raw elevation data, start to finish, in ArcGIS Pro.

TypePersonal project / practice exercise
LocationIbarapa East LGA, Oyo State, Nigeria
SoftwareArcGIS Pro (Hydrology toolset)
DataSRTM DEM, USGS EarthExplorer
5 Sub-basins delineated
4 Outlet points identified

Overview

Watershed delineation is one of those GIS skills that's easy to follow in a tutorial and harder to actually own — so this was a deliberate practice run: take a raw Digital Elevation Model with no pre-processing, and work all the way through to a clean, presentable basin map with no assignment brief telling me the steps. The area of interest is Ibarapa East, a local government area in Oyo State, Nigeria.

Data & method

Elevation data came from the SRTM DEM, downloaded through USGS EarthExplorer and clipped to the Ibarapa East boundary and its surrounding catchment. From there, the analysis followed ArcGIS Pro's standard hydrology workflow:

  • Fill — corrected spurious sinks and imperfections in the raw DEM so flow paths wouldn't terminate incorrectly.
  • Flow Direction — computed the direction of steepest descent for every cell.
  • Flow Accumulation — calculated how much upstream area drains through each cell, the basis for identifying where streams actually form.
  • Stream definition & outlet placement — thresholded the flow accumulation raster to extract the river network, then identified the points where multiple streams converge and exit the area as outlets.
  • Watershed delineation — used the outlets as pour points to delineate the contributing sub-basin for each one.
